About how I use color in webapps

Background and text colors: Either dark of light, but opposite eachother with a contrast ratio between 8:1 and 20:1 or so.

Possibly have 3 or 4 shades for text and backgrounds. Different shades for diffent contexts and importance. (lighter or darker backgrounds for text boxes, lower contrast for unimportant text, higher contrast for main text, etc) No text should have a contrast ratio <4.5 with it's background.

Personally i like all my buttons to have the same text color. when this drops the contrast ratio too low, i add a text shadow around the text to make it readable.

Main Colors

An app should have 3 or 4 "main" colors. The background or text color can also be a main color, but isn't necessarily. I'd also usually use a few shades of each of these or of other very similar hues as well.

Activity Colors

These should normally overlap with main colors. They help to aid in communication by conveying additional meaning through color. Note: Color alone is inaccessible. Ensure sufficient contrast even in grayscale and make text and labels meaningful and useful.
